Public Opinion and Political Spectrums Analysis in China

I. Project Introduction

This project aims to employ mathematical and statistical methodologies, alongside an array of natural language processing (NLP) tools, to extract and analyze public opinion on various topics across multiple social media platforms, with a current focus on Zhihu, the Chinese equivalent of Quora.

Subsequently, the results will be compared with government narratives by analyzing internal government references, public documents, and news from state-owned media and press.

The primary objectives are to delineate the spectrum of public opinion, identify discrepancies between public sentiment and the government’s perspective, and, crucially, to understand the underlying factors contributing to these discrepancies.

II. Project Objectives and Questions

Project Objectives Project Questions
To comprehend the spectrum of public opinions in China Is the disparity between public opinion and government narrative a result of the government’s inability to accurately understand public needs?
To identify the discrepancy between public sentiment and official government narratives. Is this disparity a consequence of the government's deliberate neglect of public sentiment?
To investigate the underlying mechanisms contributing to this discrepancy. If the neglect is intentional, does it reflect the government's priorities or reveal underlying concerns?
